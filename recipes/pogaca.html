Pogaca (Feta Dumplings)

Pogaca is a popular Turkish pastry, soft and fluffy, typically filled with ingredients such as cheese, potatoes, or olives.

These savory buns are enjoyed as a snack or breakfast item and are known for their tender, buttery texture.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Baking powder
  • Canola oil
  • Butter, melted
  • Plain yogurt
  • Beaten eggs
  • Sour cream
  • White sugar
  • Salt
  • Crumbled feta cheese
  • Finely chopped fresh parsley
  • Olive oil
  • Paprika
  • Egg yolks
  • Sesame seeds

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Mix the flour with baking soda in a bowl; set aside.
  2. Mix the canola oil, butter, yogurt, eggs, sour cream, sugar, and salt together in a large mixing bowl until smooth. Gradually stir the flour mixture into the wet ingredients to form a soft dough.
  3. Knead and squeeze the dough in the bowl until it is no longer sticky, about 5 minutes. Stir the feta cheese, parsley, olive oil, and paprika together in a bowl with a fork to make a filling.
  4. Make each dumpling by pinching off about 2 1/2 tablespoons of dough; roll into a ball about 1 3/4 inches across between your hands, and press it into a flat circle 3 inches across on your palm.
  5. Spoon 1 scant tablespoon of filling into the center of the circle. Bring the edges of the circle together over the filling, and pinch them together to make a fat, round little stuffed dumpling.
  6. Set the dumplings onto the parchment paper-lined baking sheet, seam sides down.
  7. Beat the egg yolks in a small bowl, and brush the pogaca tops with the egg yolk. Sprinkle each with about 1/4 teaspoon of sesame seeds.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven until the tops are shiny, brown, and crackled, 20 to 30 minutes.
